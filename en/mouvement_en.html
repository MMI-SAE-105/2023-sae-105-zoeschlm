Art movement

Using the pop-art methods of the 1960s and drawing inspiration from the works of Roy Lichtenstein and Andy Warhol, Neo-pop-art appeared on the New York art scene at the end of the 1980s.

NEO

POP-ART

We talk to you about it.

Closer to the people than Pop Art, like Free Figuration, Neo Pop refutes the idea that one Art is superior to others and aims to demonstrate that popular culture, the consumer society and Art are on the same floor and can coexist in the same image. On the contrary, they advocate an art close to the people and their buyers. It is often compared to new illustration, which is generally less assertive. Our artists know how to emphasize the contrast of both bright and soft colors to create a certain atmosphere. According to Jean-Luc Chalumeau, “The New Pops are faithful to this state of mind, and reveal themselves as a generation of artists all endowed with impeccable technical means and practicing subtle shifts in their respective ways of questioning reality. Without complex vis-à-vis the protective shadows of Warhol or Liechtenstein, knowing how to inoculate them with various doses of Narrative Figuration or Mangas, they are above all painters of their time”1.
